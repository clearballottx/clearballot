Why am I voting again? What is a Primary Runoff?

Texas holds its regular primary elections in March. If no candidate in a race receives more than 50% of the vote, the top two finishers advance to a Primary Runoff — a second election to produce a clear majority winner. That's this election. The winner of each party's runoff will appear on the November general election ballot as that party's official nominee. You vote in the same party's runoff as the primary you participated in. If you didn't vote in March, you can participate in either party's runoff — but not both.

United States Senator
Federal · 6-year term
About This Office ↗
What Does a U.S. Senator Do? Texas sends two senators to Washington to represent all Texans. Together with the full 100-member Senate they vote on federal laws, confirm presidential appointments (judges, cabinet members), ratify treaties, and control the federal budget.
November General Election: Winner faces Democratic nominee James Talarico (Texas State Representative, Austin).

Qualifications

Texas Senator since 2003, currently his fourth term. Previously served as Texas District Court Judge, Texas Supreme Court Justice, and Texas Attorney General (1999–2002). Law degree from St. Mary's University; LL.M. from the University of Virginia. Has served as Senate Majority Whip and Senate Majority Leader.

Career Record & Priorities

Heritage Action scores: 35% (118th Congress), 47–84% range across prior congresses — solidly conservative but among the more moderate Senate Republicans by that scorecard. ACU lifetime score ~93%. Focuses on border security, Texas energy, lower taxes, veterans' benefits, housing supply.

Ken Paxton
Qualifications

Texas Attorney General since 2015 (three terms). Before that, served ten years in the Texas House of Representatives and two years in the Texas Senate. Law degree from the University of Virginia. As AG, led multi-state lawsuits challenging federal executive actions.

ACU named him a leading conservative during his Texas legislative career (2003–2015). As AG: filed 100+ lawsuits challenging the Biden administration; led multi-state ACA challenge to the Supreme Court. Opposes U.S. military aid to Ukraine. Supports strict immigration enforcement. Prioritizes social conservative policies and reducing federal spending.

Texas Railroad Commissioner
Statewide · 6-year term
About This Office ↗
What Does the Texas Railroad Commissioner Do? Despite the name, this office hasn't regulated railroads since 2005. The three-member Texas Railroad Commission regulates oil and gas production, natural gas pipelines and utilities, pipeline safety, coal and uranium mining, and management of abandoned oil wells. Texas produces more oil than all other states combined — one of the most economically powerful state regulatory positions in the country.
November General Election: Winner faces Democrat Jon Rosenthal (Texas State Representative, Houston).
Jim WrightIncumbent
Qualifications

Railroad Commissioner since January 2021. Before politics, owned and operated oilfield waste management companies in South Texas — direct industry experience in the sector he now regulates. Endorsed by Governor Greg Abbott.

Commission Record & Priorities

Passed the first overhaul of oilfield waste (produced water) rules in 40 years — a documented commission vote supported by landowner groups, opposed by some major oil companies who filed suit. Oversaw commission's new authority over hydrogen storage and CO₂ injection. Advocates converting produced water into a drought solution for Texas communities.

Bo French
Qualifications

Former Chairman of the Tarrant County Republican Party (resigned Nov. 2025 to run). His family owns French Oil Company, a Midland-based oil business. No engineering credential, law degree, or direct regulatory experience. Background is political party organizing and family oil operations.

Stated Priorities

Eliminate "radical climate ideology" at the commission; roll back environmental regulations; restrict foreign investment in Texas energy assets; eliminate DEI programs within the agency. Note: Multiple opponents and energy experts noted several of these priorities fall outside the Railroad Commission's statutory authority.

About This Office ↗
What Does the Texas Court of Criminal Appeals Do? Texas's highest court for all criminal matters — the final word on every criminal appeal, including all death penalty cases. The nine elected judges set binding precedent for every criminal court in Texas, determining rules of evidence, sentencing validity, and the rights of defendants and crime victims statewide. This seat is open because Judge Bert Richardson retired.
November General Election: Winner faces Democrat Okey Anyiam (criminal defense attorney).
Alison Fox
Qualifications

B.A. from UT Austin; law degree from St. Mary's University. Worked as a prosecutor in the Bexar County DA's Office, leading the Ethical Disclosure Unit and the Conviction Integrity Unit — which reviews potentially wrongful convictions. Currently a staff attorney at the Texas Court of Criminal Appeals itself. Finished first in the primary with ~31.3%.

Career Record & Priorities

Career spans both prosecution and conviction review — documented experience on both sides. Pledges judicial neutrality, consistent application of criminal law, and upholding victims' rights. Endorsed by multiple Texas newspaper editorial boards and Texas Right to Life PAC.

Thomas Smith
Qualifications

Assistant Attorney General in the Texas AG's office. Previously worked in Ken Paxton's Texas Senate office. Advanced from a four-candidate primary with ~30.7%. No prior judicial experience on public record.

Career Record & Priorities

Entire career in prosecution-side and executive branch roles. Pledges strict constitutional interpretation of criminal law, support for victims' rights, and a tough-on-crime philosophy. Detailed published platform was limited at press time.

What Does the Texas Lieutenant Governor Do? Often described as the most powerful office in Texas state government. The Lt. Gov. presides over the Texas Senate, controls which bills come to the floor for a vote, appoints all Senate committee chairs, and largely sets the legislative agenda. Focuses on public school funding, healthcare, housing, water infrastructure.
